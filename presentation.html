Slide 1 — Problem

The data already exists. It just isn't surfaced.

A multi-tenant MSSP SOC serving many partners and customers has no single place to see how it's actually performing.

Zero visibility
Leadership and customers get a static report, not a live view of alert volume, SLA compliance, or noise.
Alert fatigue
Without a false-positive rate surfaced, analysts drown in noise no one is measuring.
Silent SLA risk
A breach is only visible after it's already happened — too late to intervene.
Trust gap
Every partner/customer needs their own scoped view — one dashboard for everyone isn't safe or credible.

Slide 2 — Architecture

PulseSOC — one process, many logical services, RBAC enforced server-side

Logically split, physically one FastAPI process — same code, N containers whenever it's worth it. Zero third-party runtime dependencies: no chart library, no date library, no email/Teams SDK.

Slide 3 — Live Flow

Same dashboard. Different role. Watch the data narrow.

Tenant scope comes from the JWT, not from anything the client sends.

Login UI→ POST /api/auth/login→ JWT issued→ GET /api/incidents + Bearer→ RBAC filter (server-side)→ UI renders scoped view

Slide 4 — Killer Feature 1

SLA Breach Predictor — future, not past

Every open incident's SLA target, elapsed time, and countdown — recomputed live, not looked up after the fact.

4 min left · Close / Resolve Now →

  • SLA targets are configurable per partner and per customer — not hardcoded
  • <=5 min left on a Critical (or <=15 min on a Major) → blinking_critical, which drives the war-room banner and the blinking ticket chip
  • Already-breached tickets are labeled BREACHED, not HIGH — a prediction that already happened isn't a prediction

Slide 6 — Live Demo (60 seconds)

Partner Registration → SLA Config → Ticket Blink → Close to Save SLA

Zero seed data, zero pre-baked demo — built live, in front of the judges, via Demo Setup.

Register "Demo Partner"→ Create "Demo Customer"→ Set Critical SLA = 5 min→ Create Critical ticket→ Blinking in Early Warning→ Close / Resolve Now
  • Ticket appears blinking immediately — a 5-minute SLA means 5 minutes elapsed is already the whole budget
  • War Room banner flashes red at the top of the screen with a live 1-second countdown; a real .eml + Teams JSON land in /tmp/emails / /tmp/teams, visible on the Notifications page
  • Snooze it for 15 min → banner clears, ticket marked SNOOZED; wait it out and the 60s auto-escalation loop picks it back up on its own
  • Closing it before the buzzer: toast "SLA saved with N min left!" + confetti + live KPI refresh, no reload
  • Every step logged to logs/flow.log — partner registered, SLA configured, ticket created, SLA saved
