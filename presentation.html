Slide 1 — Problem

The data already exists. It just isn't surfaced.

A multi-tenant MSSP SOC serving many partners and customers has no single place to see how it's actually performing.

Zero visibility
Leadership and customers get a static report, not a live view of alert volume, SLA compliance, or noise.
Alert fatigue
Without a false-positive rate surfaced, analysts drown in noise no one is measuring.
Silent SLA risk
A breach is only visible after it's already happened — too late to intervene.
Trust gap
Every partner/customer needs their own scoped view — one dashboard for everyone isn't safe or credible.

Slide 3 — Live Flow

Same dashboard. Different role. Watch the data narrow.

Tenant scope comes from the JWT, not from anything the client sends.

Login UI→ POST /api/auth/login→ JWT issued→ GET /api/incidents + Bearer→ RBAC filter (server-side)→ UI renders scoped view

Demo sequence

  • superadmin → all partners, all customers
  • partner_mgr → partner-a only, customer dropdown narrows
  • customer_viewer → one customer, no partner picker at all
  • Direct-URL /admin as customer_viewer → 403, visible live in /flow

Slide 4 — Trends, Drill-down & Early Warning

Beyond the KPI cards

Two weekly trend charts, incident-level drill-down, and a live SLA-breach early warning.

  • Volume trend — alerts vs. incidents, weekly, showing the funnel narrowing
  • MTTR vs SLA trend — dual-axis: resolution speed against compliance, side by side
  • Drill-down — click any incident for MITRE technique, assigned analyst, and a category-specific recommendation
  • Early warning (bonus) — a P1 at risk banner surfaces any Critical incident open 3+ hours, before it crosses the 4-hour SLA breach threshold — the difference between reacting to a breach and preventing one
